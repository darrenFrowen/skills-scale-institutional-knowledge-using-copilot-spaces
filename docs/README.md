# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme follows an iterative, customer-focused, and data-informed project management methodology designed to deliver value quickly while maintaining quality and transparency. Every project moves through a structured lifecycle—Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective—with clear ownership at each stage. A named Project Manager (PM) coordinates delivery, schedules, and communications, while the Product Manager defines outcomes and priorities. Developers, QA engineers, and stakeholders each have defined responsibilities that promote accountability and collaboration across teams.

Communication is central to OctoAcme's approach. Teams hold twice-weekly standups, weekly PM–Product Manager syncs, and monthly stakeholder updates to keep everyone aligned. Risks are tracked in a shared Risk Register from the earliest planning stages and reviewed at each weekly sync. Escalation paths are clearly defined—from the team level up through the PM, Product Lead, and Sponsor—so that blockers are resolved quickly and decisions are made at the right level. Stakeholder groups receive regular, templated status updates that surface progress, blockers, and decisions needed.

Quality and release practices are treated as first-class concerns. Acceptance criteria and a Definition of Done are established during planning and enforced by QA before any release. Deployments follow a standardized release process with a documented incident playbook to handle unexpected issues. After each cycle, the team conducts a blameless retrospective to capture learnings and produce actionable improvements, ensuring that processes and documentation stay current and continuously evolve.

All process documents in this folder are living resources maintained by the team. They provide the authoritative reference for how OctoAcme initiates, plans, executes, and closes projects. New team members are encouraged to read the Overview first and then explore the topic-specific guides below to understand how each phase works and how their role fits into the broader workflow.
