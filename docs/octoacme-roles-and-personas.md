# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates Agile ceremonies, coaches the team on process adherence, and removes impediments so the team can deliver at a sustainable pace.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and escalate impediments that cannot be resolved at the team level
- Coach team members on Agile principles and continuous improvement practices
- Shield the team from external distractions during a sprint
- Track and communicate sprint health (velocity, burndown)

### Goals
- Keep the team focused, unblocked, and consistently delivering
- Foster a culture of transparency and continuous improvement
- Uphold agreed-upon processes and Definition of Done

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Impediment log and blockers board
- Retrospective action items and follow-ups

### How this role interacts with existing roles
- **Developers**: Acts as a coach and facilitator, removing blockers and protecting the team's focus during sprints.
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates to ensure the backlog is refined and prioritized before sprint planning; escalates scope conflicts promptly.
- **Project Managers**: Shares sprint health metrics and raises cross-team dependencies or risks that affect the project timeline.

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for the user experience by translating user research and business goals into intuitive designs. They bridge the gap between user needs and technical implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct or synthesize user research to inform design decisions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Validate designs through usability testing and iteration
- Define and maintain design standards and component libraries
- Communicate design rationale and trade-offs to the team

### Goals
- Ensure the product is usable, accessible, and delightful for end users
- Reduce rework caused by late-stage usability issues
- Align user experience with product vision and business outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and prototype walkthroughs
- Annotated mockups and design specs shared with Developers
- User research summaries shared with Product Managers

### How this role interacts with existing roles
- **Developers**: Provides design specs and assets; collaborates on feasibility trade-offs and participates in PR reviews for UI changes.
- **Product Managers**: Works closely to translate product requirements into user-centred designs; validates that designs meet defined success metrics.
- **Project Managers**: Flags design dependencies and timelines that may affect the project schedule; participates in milestone demos.

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical delivery. They clarify requirements, document process flows, and ensure that what is built aligns with business outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements and acceptance criteria
- Create process flows, data models, and use-case documentation
- Identify and clarify ambiguities in requirements before development begins
- Support backlog refinement by translating business needs into user stories
- Validate that delivered features meet business requirements

### Goals
- Reduce rework caused by unclear or misunderstood requirements
- Ensure traceability from business goal to delivered feature
- Facilitate shared understanding across business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and review sessions
- Written user stories, acceptance criteria, and process flow documentation
- Clarification threads and decision logs during development

### How this role interacts with existing roles
- **Developers**: Provides detailed requirements and acceptance criteria; answers clarifying questions during development and participates in acceptance testing.
- **Product Managers**: Partners to translate product vision into detailed, actionable requirements; flags scope gaps or conflicts early.
- **Project Managers**: Reports on requirements readiness and flags any open questions that could affect the project timeline.

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead oversees quality practices across the project lifecycle, from defining test strategies to coordinating test execution and reporting. They are the primary point of contact for quality-related matters.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy and test plan
- Coordinate test planning, execution, and reporting across the team
- Establish and uphold the Definition of Done with regard to quality
- Oversee automated and manual testing (unit, integration, end-to-end, and acceptance)
- Triage defects and manage the defect lifecycle
- Act as the escalation point for test-related issues

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release
- Reduce defect escape rate to production
- Build a sustainable, efficient test suite that supports continuous delivery

### Typical Communication
- Test plans, test reports, and defect summaries
- Participation in sprint planning and reviews to clarify acceptance criteria
- Escalation of critical defects to the Project Manager and Product Manager

### How this role interacts with existing roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on testability, reviews automated tests, and works jointly to triage and resolve defects.
- **Product Managers**: Validates acceptance criteria and aligns on Definition of Done; reports on quality metrics that inform release decisions.
- **Project Managers**: Provides quality status reports; flags testing risks or blockers that may affect release timelines.

---

## Stakeholder Representative

### Role Summary
The Stakeholder Representative acts as the voice of key business or customer groups within the project team. They ensure that stakeholder perspectives are reflected in decisions and that deliverables meet stakeholder expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Communicate stakeholder needs, priorities, and constraints to the project team
- Review and provide timely feedback on deliverables and milestones
- Participate in demos, user acceptance testing, and sign-offs
- Escalate concerns or misalignments from the stakeholder community
- Facilitate stakeholder communication and manage expectations

### Goals
- Ensure the project outcomes align with stakeholder needs and organizational goals
- Enable timely feedback loops so issues are caught before release
- Build confidence and trust between the delivery team and stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Monthly (or milestone-driven) stakeholder updates
- Feedback sessions following demos and reviews
- Written sign-off on major deliverables

### How this role interacts with existing roles
- **Developers**: Participates in demos to provide direct feedback; avoids bypassing the Product Manager or Project Manager for ad-hoc scope requests.
- **Product Managers**: Primary relationship for translating stakeholder needs into backlog priorities and product decisions.
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on communication cadence, milestone sign-offs, and escalation paths for unresolved concerns.
